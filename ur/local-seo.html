Local SEO

Get found by customers searching nearby

Local SEO is what gets your business into Google Maps and the local pack — so the people already searching for what you offer in your area find you, not a competitor.

What is Local SEO

Visibility where it matters — on Search and Maps

When someone searches "restaurant near me" or "dentist in Gulshan" on their phone, Google shows a map with three local businesses and a list below it. That's the local pack — and it drives an enormous share of calls, direction requests and walk-ins.

Local SEO is the process of improving your ranking in that local pack and the organic results below it. It's different from general SEO: it's specifically about proximity, relevance to local searches, and the trust signals that tell Google your business is a legitimate, active operation in that area.

Ranking signal groups

What Google uses to rank local businesses

Google weighs signals across eight groups when deciding which businesses appear first in local results. Findry works across all of them.

Google Business Profile signals

Your GBP is the single most influential asset in local search. An optimised, verified profile with accurate categories, real photos and regular posts signals that your business is active and relevant.

CategoryPhotosPostsHoursServicesCompleteness

Review signals

Reviews tell Google how customers experience your business. Volume, freshness, star rating and thoughtful responses all influence how your listing ranks — and what potential customers decide.

QuantityRecencyStar ratingResponsesKeywords in reviews

On-page signals

Your website reinforces your Google presence. Local landing pages, correct headings, schema markup, mobile performance and clear NAP (name, address, phone) all strengthen your ranking in local results.

Local keywordsSchema markupMobile speedNAP consistencyTitle tags

Citation signals

Every time your business name, address and phone appear consistently across the web, Google's confidence in your data grows. Inconsistencies across directories undermine that trust and suppress your ranking.

Name/address/phoneDirectory coverageConsistencyLocal portals

Link signals

Links from local publications, chambers of commerce, supplier sites and relevant associations tell Google that real people in your area recognise and trust your business.

Local pressChamber linksSupplier linksRelevant directories

Behavioural signals

When people click, call and request directions from local results, it signals relevance. These are influences on ranking — not direct guarantees — but optimising the signals within our control contributes to sustained visibility.

ClicksCallsDirection requestsWebsite visits

Personalisation signals

Google personalises results based on user location, device, time of day and search history. Consistent, optimised visibility across all signals gives you the best chance of appearing across those variations.

User locationDeviceTime of dayProximity

Social & discovery signals

Active social profiles, branded searches and local engagement support discovery and build trust with potential customers. These support — not replace — core local SEO work.

Profile activityBranded searchesLocal engagementGBP posts
